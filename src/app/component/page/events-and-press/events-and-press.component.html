January 2, 2017

We are pleased to announce the next PSL seminar which will take place on Tuesday January 17, from 12:15 p.m. to 1:30 p.m. at Paris-Dauphine University, room C108.

« Health science and data science. ‘New’ methods of accessing and processing health data. »

Big data, smart data, data science... are all terms that have spread to the public and different scientific fields in a few years. The fields of health and biomedical research in particular have been interested in it since 2008, and the number of publications on “big data” in health has increased exponentially since 2012. However, we are still essentially in the time of promise. which remain to be concretized and evaluated. In France, in particular, there are still relatively few concrete initiatives relating to health data science. Among these initiatives, most are currently carried out by the hospital environment, whether in Ile de France (APHP) or in the Greater West (CHRU of Brest, Rennes). Here we present the context of big data in health, as well as two projects currently being deployed in hospitals. The health data warehouse project supported by the APHP and covering data from all APHP establishments will also be presented. Other projects in the short and medium term will be outlined. The speakers:

  • T. Lefèvre, APHP, Iris INSERM/CNRS/EHESS/UP13, coordinator of the big data ministerial think tank in health
  • A. Lichterowicz, Tekliko (PME Paris, Singapore)
  • R. Beaufret, APHP, leads the APHP WIND (Web Innovations in Health Data) team

Entry is free but registration is required (by January 12 at noon at the latest). Sandwiches will be provided.
